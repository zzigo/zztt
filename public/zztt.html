My name is Luciano Azzigotti. Although originally trained as a pianist and composer, my work has encompassed concert music, electronic arts, internet language research, curation, writing, teaching, and filmscoring. I also collaborated with other artists in dance and visual arts. I am currently working on my doctoral thesis, which deals with speculative ideas about music and instruments.

In this notion, there are past, present and future works. Updates are posted through Instagram

science and music

How can numerical thinking contribute new mental models for thinking about music? What are the points of common emergence between the history of music and its tangible and intangible artifacts? Is it music that predicts scientific paradigms, or can the speculative-mathematical method of science construct musical meaning?
